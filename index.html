About

Alexei Hoffman is a Research Associate with the Aerospace Security Project at the Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), where he focuses on unmanned-systems policy and counter-UAS doctrine. His work examines how threat environments, acquisition priorities, and alliance structures shape the aerospace and defense landscape across Eurasia and the Indo-Pacific. A first-generation American of Russian and Kazakh descent, he brings linguistic fluency in Russian and Mandarin to his analysis of great-power competition. His research has been published in the Georgetown Security Studies Review and Military Review, among other outlets, and he was named a 2026 Rising Expert by Young Professionals in Foreign Policy.

Before joining CSIS, Alexei served as a Global Government Relations Specialist at Collins Aerospace (RTX) and as a China Policy Research Analyst at the Institute for the Study of War, contributing to weekly assessments on PLA activity and Indo-Pacific security developments distributed to government and military stakeholders.

Alexei is a candidate for the Master of Asian Studies at Georgetown University's Walsh School of Foreign Service, with a concentration in International Political Economy, and holds a Certificate in Diplomatic Studies. He holds a J.D. from Georgia State University College of Law and an M.A. in International Politics and Military Affairs from The Citadel. Before graduate school, he served as a Joint Fire Support Specialist in the United States Army's 10th Mountain Division, deploying to Afghanistan and conducting partner-force advisory missions in Kazakhstan, South Korea, and Japan. He subsequently spent nearly a decade in national security and government matters at King & Spalding LLP before volunteering in 2023 as a defense training advisor with the International Legion for the Territorial Defense of Ukraine.
