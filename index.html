About

Alexei Hoffman is a Research Associate with the Aerospace Security Project at the Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), where he focuses on unmanned-systems policy and counter-UAS doctrine. His work examines how threat environments, acquisition priorities, and alliance structures shape the aerospace and defense landscape, with particular focus on how near-peer competition, supply-chain realities, and regulatory frameworks drive strategic outcomes across Eurasia and the Indo-Pacific.

A first-generation American of Russian and Kazakh descent, Alexei brings linguistic fluency in Russian and Mandarin and deep regional expertise to his analysis of great-power competition. Before joining CSIS, he served as a Global Government Relations Specialist at Collins Aerospace (RTX), where he tracked defense authorization and appropriations provisions across four congressional committees and co-authored policy memos on defense-trade controls and allied co-production developments for government affairs leadership. He also served as a China Policy Research Analyst at the Institute for the Study of War, contributing to weekly assessments on PLA activity, CCP coercion campaigns, and Indo-Pacific security developments distributed to government and military consumers.

Alexei is a candidate for the Master of Asian Studies at Georgetown University's Walsh School of Foreign Service, with a concentration in International Political Economy, and holds a Certificate in Diplomatic Studies from Georgetown's Institute for the Study of Diplomacy. He holds a J.D. and Certificate in Intellectual Property Law from Georgia State University College of Law, and an M.A. in International Politics and Military Affairs from The Citadel. His research has been published in the Georgetown Security Studies Review, Military Review, and through CSIS and Georgetown's Institute for the Study of Diplomacy, among other outlets. He was named a 2026 Rising Expert by Young Professionals in Foreign Policy.

Before graduate school, Alexei served as a Joint Fire Support Specialist in the United States Army's 10th Mountain Division, deploying to Afghanistan and conducting partner-force advisory and training missions in Kazakhstan, South Korea, and Japan. He subsequently spent nearly a decade in national security and government matters at King & Spalding LLP, supporting CFIUS reviews, defense-sector M&A, and export-control compliance. In 2023, he volunteered as a defense training advisor with the International Legion for the Territorial Defense of Ukraine.
